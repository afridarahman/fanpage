The Life of Beebo

Brendon Urie, sometimes known as Beebo, was born on April 12, 1987 in St. George, Utah. In 2004, Urie formed the band Panic! at the Disco as lead singer with Ryan Ross, Spencer Smith, and Brent Wilson, who was eventually fired in 2006 and replaced by Jon Walker. They debuted their first album "A Fever You Can't Sweat Out" on September 27, 2005. In 2009, co-singer Ryan Ross and Jon Walker separated from Panic! and formed their own band called "The Young Veins". Panic! had released four albums ( "A Fever You Can't Sweat Out"(2005), "Pretty. Odd."(2008), "Vices and Virtues"(2011), and "Too Weird to Live, Too Rare to Die"(2013)) before band member Spencer Smith announced he was leaving the band to fight his drug addiction, leaving Brendon Urie as the only member of Panic!. Since then, Urie has released two self-produced albums called "Death of a Bachelor"(2016) and "Pray for the Wicked"(2018). Urie was casted for the hit broadway show "Kinky Boots" in 2017. This year, Urie performed his hit song "High Hopes" from his recent album at the VMA's. Urie has only won one award (VMA Video of the Year Award in 2006), but has been nominated for 7 VMA awards and more. In 2014, Urie married Sarah Orzechowski and has written songs about her such as "Sarah Smiles" and "End of all Things".
